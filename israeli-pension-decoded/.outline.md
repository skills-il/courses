## Chapters (6)

### Chapter 1: What pension actually buys you
- Three products in one: retirement income + disability insurance + survivor benefits
- Israeli pension landscape: keren pensia vs. bituach menahalim vs. kupat gemel — what each is
- Why "deal with pension later" costs real money (lost employer match + lost tax benefit)
- Decision anchor: 2026 average wage ₪13,769, pension cap ₪50,695
- Recommended skill mention: `israeli-pension-advisor` for follow-up Q&A
- Most common mistake: confusing keren pensia with kupat gemel

### Chapter 2: The two-question decision tree (the framework)
- Q1 — Do you need a pension fund right now?
  - Decision tree branches: employee day 1 (with prior coverage), employee month 7 (first job), self-employed (mandatory since 2017)
  - Edge case: students/part-time/contractors
- Q2 — Who inherits if something happens to you tomorrow?
  - Spouse alive + minor children → spouse + orphans get monthly pension
  - Spouse alive, no kids → spouse gets pension subject to income test
  - Single + minor children → orphans get pension
  - Single, no kids → designated beneficiary OR legal heirs (court order)
- Most common mistake: ~30% of Israelis never update their beneficiary form after marriage/divorce/kids
- Worked example: married, 2 kids, no will → what happens vs. married, will + updated beneficiary → what happens

### Chapter 3: Why you got auto-assigned to a default fund
- The 4 default funds 2021-2028 and the policy intent (regulated fees, broad access)
- Auto-allocation by ID check-digit (June 2025+, employers 50+) — concrete table
- Why "default" ≠ "best": it means "regulated cap on fees, decent enough for hands-off"
- Fees during the default period: 0.22% accumulation + 1% deposits
- Recommended skill mention: `israeli-bituach-leumi` for old-age pension (kiztavat zikna) layer
- Most common mistake: assuming the default is also the best long-term — the default is GOOD for fees, AVERAGE for returns

### Chapter 4: How to actually compare funds (fees + returns)
- The official portal: פנסיה נט (pensianet.cma.gov.il)
- The 6 columns that matter: deposit fee %, accumulation fee %, 1y return, 3y return, 5y return, 5y cumulative
- Why 5-year matters more than 1-year (smooths market noise)
- Israeli-specific constraint: Erev regulations mandate government bond holdings, which caps equity allocation → returns look lower than US 401(k)
- 2025 leaders (5-year cumulative, age-based tracks): Phoenix, Clal, Meitav
- 2025 single-year equity track leader: Menorah Mivtachim 21.8% (5y 81.4%)
- Worked example: ₪10k/mo contribution, default fees (0.22% + 1%) vs. typical non-default (0.5% + 2.5%) over 30 years
- Most common mistake: chasing last year's winner instead of looking at 5-year cumulative

### Chapter 5: Self-employed pension (mandatory since 2017)
- Why it exists: pre-2017 self-employed retirees were disproportionately poor
- 2026 rates: 4.45% up to ₪82,614, 12.55% from ₪82,615 to ₪165,228 (= half avg wage and full avg wage)
- 2026 tax benefits:
  - Up to ₪38,412 deductible (16.5% of the ₪232,800 income cap)
  - Up to ₪12,804 contribution eligible for 35% credit (worth up to ₪4,481 in tax saved)
- Common mistake: paying just the minimum to avoid the penalty (₪500/year), missing the tax benefit ladder
- Pairing strategy: keren pensia + keren hishtalmut for full tax stack
- Recommended skill mention: `israeli-pension-advisor` (covers keren hishtalmut + bituach menahalim)
- Worked example: ₪200k/year freelancer, mandatory minimum vs. optimized contribution → tax saved

### Chapter 6: When to switch funds (and when to stay put)
- Switch reasons that hold up: fees > 1% on deposit OR 5-year return >2 pp below cohort
- Switch reasons that DON'T hold up: one bad quarter, hot tip, employer pressure
- The "I want to leave it all to my adult children" mistake — adult kids only get residual lump sum, not the survivor pension
- Switching costs: timing the move (mid-month vs. mid-year), insurance underwriting may re-open
- When to consult a licensed pension advisor (יועץ פנסיוני): inheritance planning, leaving Israel, switching mid-career
- Recommended skills: `israeli-pension-advisor` (ongoing chat), `israeli-bituach-leumi` (govt layer), `israeli-payroll-calculator` (verifying employer deductions)
- Disclaimer: this is education, not investment advice; for binding recommendations consult a licensed יועץ פנסיוני
