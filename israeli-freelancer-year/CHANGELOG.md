### English
- Corrected keren hishtalmut for self-employed 2026 caps: ₪13,203 deductible (not the previously stated ₪19,500) plus a separate ₪20,566 capital-gains-exempt deposit cap; worked example updated (35% × ₪13,203 = ₪4,621 tax saved, not ₪6,825).
- Corrected vehicle expense recognition to 45% per Income Tax Regulations (was incorrectly stated as 67% in chapter 4).
- Updated client gift expense ceiling to ₪230 per person per year (2026 indexed value, was incorrectly ₪210).
- Rewrote Section 47 self-employed pension explanation: removed inaccurate "around ₪22,000" cap, replaced with Section 47 11% deduction + Section 45a 35% credit on additional 5%, both tied to the average wage ceiling.
- Rewrote Section 187 explanation: removed inaccurate "around 5% of the gap" framing, replaced with the actual interest (4% per year per Section 159a) plus CPI linkage rule from end of tax year until payment.
- Dropped 2 unused recommended_skills entries (`israeli-payroll-calculator`, `israeli-employee-tax-refund`) that were not referenced anywhere in the course body.
- Removed the unverified ₪2,000 immediate-expense equipment threshold claim; softened equipment depreciation section to cite actual regulation rates (33% personal computers, 25% other computers, 7%-25% furniture).
- Regenerated evidence.json from scratch: 16 entries (was 6) with chapter-specific sourcing covering all numeric claims. Removed stale ev-003 (VAT cadence threshold ₪1,615,000) whose claim was absent from the course body.
- Israeli CPA persona review surfaced several real gaps now fixed: added 52% Bituach Leumi self-employed deduction (Section 47A) as a 7th deductible expense category in chapter 4; added the "gift must carry business branding" requirement; strengthened the foreign-income warning to mention Section 220 criminal exposure and CRS/FATCA cross-matching; restored the ₪2,000 equipment immediate-expense threshold as a concrete number; added the May 31 Form 1301 deadline for double-entry-book filers alongside the April 30 single-entry deadline; added Section 17 home-office audit-risk caveat; softened the osek patur "30 days" conversion to "promptly upon crossing" since no statutory grace period exists; anchored VAT 18% rate to its January 2025 effective date.
- Added a "this is not tax advice" closing note at the end of chapter 6 directing readers to a licensed CPA for foreign income, business sale, regulated professions, audits, or contested positions.
- Adjusted accountant fee range from "low- to mid-thousand-shekel" to the more realistic "₪5,000-₪10,000 per year for an active osek murshe" after persona review.
- Adjusted refund timing expectation from "4 to 8 weeks" to acknowledge that files triggering review can take 3-6 months.
