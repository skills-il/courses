## Why this passes editorial test #1

Eight skills already cover individual operations (ops alerts, VAT filing, Form 1301, pension math, payroll, Bituach Leumi programs, bank transactions, refund hunting). None teaches the strategic timing across them. A freelancer using `israeli-freelancer-ops` learns deadline alerts; they don't learn that filing a tikun mkdamot in January when Q4 came in 40% over forecast saves them the Section 187 penalty. That synthesis is the course.

## Chapter outline

### Chapter 1: How to think about the freelancer year (~10 min)

**Framework:** The Israeli freelancer year is 4 quarters with 12 fixed deadlines + 6 strategic decisions. The deadlines you cannot move; the strategic decisions you can.

**Concrete payoff:**
- One-page calendar with 12 deadlines highlighted (Jan 15 mkdamot, Apr 30 annual report, etc.)
- Decision-tree: osek patur vs osek murshe (with the 2026 threshold)
- "Reading your previous year" framework (where did you over/under-pay?)

**Common mistake covered:** Starting the year without reading last year's Tofes 6111 → repeats the same mkdamot mistakes.

**Recommended skills:** `israeli-freelancer-ops` (set up the deadline alerts), `israeli-bank-connector` (categorize last year's transactions to inform this year's decisions).

### Chapter 2: VAT cycle (~10 min)

**Framework:** Monthly vs bi-monthly Doch Maam — pick based on income predictability, not by default. The threshold + the predictability heuristic.

**Concrete payoff:**
- Worked example: a freelancer at ₪35k/month average with low variance pays bi-monthly; same income with 60% variance benefits from monthly
- "VAT acceleration" mistake: when you accidentally charge VAT on a zero-rated export
- Q2 reconciliation timing (when bi-monthly filers should pre-audit)

**Common mistake:** Filing bi-monthly out of habit when monthly would smooth cash flow; missing the deadline for switching cadence (set once per year).

**Recommended skills:** `israeli-vat-reporting` (the actual filing), `israeli-bank-connector`.

### Chapter 3: Mkdamot strategy (~12 min)

**Framework:** Mkdamot are computed from prior year. The real decision is whether to file a `tikun mkdamot` (adjustment) when current year diverges. The Section 187 penalty math.

**Concrete payoff:**
- Worked example: prior year ₪400k, current Q1+Q2 trending ₪600k → tikun in July saves ~₪3,000 in Section 187 penalties
- "Underpayment vs overpayment" decision (deferring tax payment has a known cost — what's the breakeven vs the bank's interest rate?)
- When to file vs accept

**Common mistake:** Letting mkdamot run on prior-year defaults when income jumps 30%+ → Section 187 surprise on April 30.

**Recommended skills:** `israeli-tax-returns` (the math + filing).

### Chapter 4: Expense optimization across the year (~12 min)

**Framework:** The 4 expense levers a freelancer pulls (capex timing, keren hishtalmut contribution, pension, Section 17 home office) + when in the year each one has maximum effect.

**Concrete payoff:**
- Keren hishtalmut: max annual contribution (2026: ~₪19,920) → tax saving math at marginal 35% = ~₪6,972 savings
- Pension contribution timing: contribute earlier in the year for compounding, later in the year for tax-rate certainty
- Section 17 home office: requirements + maximum
- Capex defer vs pull-forward decision tree

**Common mistake:** Maxing keren hishtalmut in December without checking marginal rate (some freelancers in low-income years over-contribute and lose the future flexibility).

**Recommended skills:** `israeli-pension-advisor`, `israeli-payroll-calculator`.

### Chapter 5: End-of-year strategy (October-December) (~12 min)

**Framework:** The last quarter is when you steer. 6 decisions to make between Oct 1 and Dec 31 that compound into your final tax bill.

**Concrete payoff:**
- Q4 income forecasting checklist (income realized + AR + invoices in flight)
- Charity donations (Section 46): donate before Dec 31, claim 35% credit, max threshold
- Last-day-of-year invoice timing (issue Jan 1 vs Dec 31 — when does each save?)
- Final mkdamot adjustment opportunity
- "Pull forward expenses" decision (buy that laptop in Dec vs Jan)

**Common mistake:** Issuing all December invoices on Dec 31 to "look big" without checking whether Jan 1 would defer income into a lower-bracket year.

**Recommended skills:** `israeli-tax-returns`, `israeli-pension-advisor`.

### Chapter 6: Tax season (January-April) (~12 min)

**Framework:** Tax season is mechanical IF you set up the year correctly. The 4-step protocol to file Form 1301 well + when to use an accountant vs do it yourself.

**Concrete payoff:**
- Documentation gathering checklist
- Refund opportunities specific to atzmai (yes — there are some that even atzmaim miss)
- Miluim refund mechanics for freelancers
- Form 1301 vs Form 1301-A (when each applies)
- Accountant decision-matrix (income ranges where DIY saves vs costs)

**Common mistake:** Filing in March because "I always do" when the freelancer's tax position would benefit from an April or May extension.

**Recommended skills:** `israeli-tax-returns`, `israeli-employee-tax-refund` (some triggers apply to atzmai too), `israeli-bituach-leumi`.
